PLANTS - WHAT ARE PLANTS?

Plants are autotrophic feeders, which means they make their own food. They go through a natural process called photosynthesis which the plant takes in CO2 e.i carbon dioxide to make oxygen which is later consumed by the animals. Plants also need sunlight from the sun , water and minerals from the soil to grow.

PLANTS - TYPES OF FLOWERS?

There are 2 types of flowers, Unisexual or bisexual, A bisexual flower has both the male and the female gametes(sex cells) example:hibiscus while, unisexual plants only have either the male gametes or the female gametes. example:cucumber

PLANTS - MALE PARTS OF A FLOWER

The sex part of the flower is called the "STAMEN", this consists the Anther and the Filament, The anther is a head-like structure which has sticky pollen on it. The filament is a slim and long tube that holds the anther in place.

PLANTS - FEMALE PARTS OF A FLOWER

This includes the style , filament , ovary and the ovules which awaits the pollen in the ovary.

LESSON NOTES.

Plants are autotrophic feeders which means they make their own food.

Plants generate chemical energy.

Unisexual plants are plants with only the male part or the female part.

Bisexual plants on the contrary, are plants with both the male and female parts together.
